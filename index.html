Personal data protection priorities

Strong Passwords

Choose passwords that consist of a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable personal information such as birthdates.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Enable two-factor authentication for your digital accounts, where you're asked to enter an additional security code after entering your password to ensure secure access.

Regular Software and App Updates

Keep your operating system, software, and apps updated regularly. Security updates patch vulnerabilities that could be exploited by attackers.

Secure Wi-Fi Networks and Avoiding Unsecured Networks

Encrypt your home Wi-Fi network and avoid conducting sensitive transactions over unsecured wireless networks to protect your data from unauthorized access and interceptions.

Review App Permissions

Make sure to review the permissions requested by apps before installing them, ensuring they don't request capabilities that are unrelated to the app's function.

The meaning and importance of cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is of paramount importance as it encompasses measures taken to protect digital systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, cyberattacks, and breaches. It plays a crucial role in safeguarding sensitive information, preserving the integrity of digital infrastructure, and ensuring the privacy and security of individuals and organizations in an increasingly interconnected digital world.

Definition of Cybersecurity

It is a field that focuses on protecting digital systems, networks, and data from electronic threats and breaches.

Cyber Threats

Cyber threats include viruses, spyware, malware, fraudulent attacks, and infrastructure-harming attacks.

Awareness and Training

User awareness of cyber risks and training on proper security practices are essential parts of cybersecurity strategies.

Laws and Regulations

There are laws and regulations aimed at enhancing cybersecurity and protecting personal data, such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the European Union.

Hacking methods

Malware Attacks

These attacks involve the use of malicious software such as viruses, spyware, and ransomware to penetrate systems, steal data, or cause damage to systems

Phishing Attacks

These attacks utilize fake emails or text messages that lure individuals into clicking on malicious links or providing sensitive personal information, aiming to steal personal information or login credentials.

Insider Threats

These refer to threats originating from within an organization or entity. The attacker may be internal, such as a former or current employee, who utilizes their insider knowledge of the system or data to engage in malicious activities such as stealing or leaking information.

Spoofing Attacks

Attackers falsify sender information in emails or web addresses to deceive recipients into providing personal information or directing them to unsafe websites.
